The Investment

Chandler Lofts is a 392-bed Class A student housing and mixed use retail development comprised of three buildings (Buildings 1, 2 and 3) located near Michigan State University. Construction is to begin during the summer of 2017, with Building 1 delivered and ready for occupancy in Fall of 2018. Buildings 2 and 3 are to be delivered and ready for occupancy in Summer of 2019. The project includes 23,217 SF in Building 1.

Investment Specialists - Chandler Lofts, LLC (the “Company”) is seeking a total of $1,650,000 in equity investments from accredited investors. It is estimated that the project will require a total equity investment of $8,258,267 of which the Sponsor is contributing $6,635,267.

The minimum investment for the offering is $25,000 for Class B member units.

Additional Capital Contributions:

The Manager may elect to seek additional capital from the Members by delivery to each Member a written notice (a “Contribution Notice”) specifying the aggregate amount of additional equity the Company is required to contribute to Chandler Lofts. Each Member shall have ten (10) calendar days from the date of receipt of the Contribution Notice to contribute his, her, or its share of the additional capital to the Company. Each Member shall receive a credit to his, her, or its Capital Amount in the amount of any additional capital that he, she, or it contributes to the Company.

Should a Member not make the contributions of additional capital, the manager may exercise the following remedies:

  • Amounts that a non-defaulting Member so advances on behalf of the Defaulting Member shall become a loan due and payable from the Defaulting Member to such non-defaulting Member and bear interest at the rate of ten percent (10%) compounded per annum, payable monthly, and are payable in full within 1 year and secured by the Defaulting Member’s units.
  • Defaulting Member’s interest in the Company shall be reduced by the Default Percentage, equal to the percentage that the defaulting Member’s unpaid capital contribution would constitute of the aggregate unreturned capital contributions of all members had it been contributed. The reduction in interest will be distributed amongst the non defaulting members in proportion to the amount of additional capital contributed.
  • The non-defaulting Members may dissolve the Company, in which event the Company shall be wound-up, liquidated, and terminated pursuant to ARTICLE XI.
  • The Company or the non-defaulting Members may purchase all of the defaulting Member’s Units, and the purchase price shall be an amount equal to eighty percent (80%) of the purchase price determined in accordance with Section 10.2. (e) of the Operating Agreement, which utilizes the fair market value of the Member’s Capital Account balance.
  • The defaulting Member will not receive any Distributions, including any Priority Return, until the non-defaulting Members have first received Distributions equal to the additional capital contributed to the Company plus a cumulative, non-compounded return thereon at the rate of ten percent (10%) per annum.
  • The defaulting Member shall lose the ability to actively participate in the Company’s management and operations until such time as the defaulting Member cures the default.
  • The defaulting Member shall lose its voting and approval rights under the Act, the Articles, and this Agreement until such time as the defaulting Member cures the default.

For a complete explanation of additional capital contribution requirements, please review Section 5.2(a) and Section 5.3(b) of the Operating Agreement.

Building 1 is to be developed with construction financing provided by MSU Federal Credit Union at the lesser of 75% Loan-to-Value or 75% Loan-to-Cost. WestPac is projecting that the loan amount will be $9,129,958 with three (3) years of interest-only payments floating at Prime. Due to the nature of the leasing cycle of student housing, WestPac anticipates that Building 1 will be fully leased prior to the completion of construction.

Provided Westpac pre-leases Building 1 by the summer of 2018, it should enable them to refinance itwith FNME or FMHC for an additional three (3) years of interest-only payments at 80% Loan-to-Value with the expectation to do a cash-out refinance of nearly $3.4 million of cash. This cash would be used towards satisfying a portion of the equity requirement to develop Buildings 2 & 3 with similar construction financing terms that were obtained to develop Building 1.




While the construction loan on each building is anticipated to be capped at 75% LTC, the re-use of Building 1 proceeds for Buildings 2 & 3 will take leverage on originally invested equity to an estimated 82% LTC. It is WestPac’s objective to sell all three buildings at the end of the third year in the fall of 2020.

The Business Plan

Located at Chandler Crossings, an area well-known as a central hub for off-campus student housing at Michigan State University, Chandler Lofts is well positioned with its Class A amenities and cutting edge design. Based on WestPac’s extensive experience in the sub-market, having seven properties in the Chandler Crossings area, and independent third-party market research, there is evidence of pent-up demand for smaller unit types (studios, one-bedroom, and two-bedroom units) that is not currently being met by existing product. Chandler Lofts is positioned to fill this demand and though commanding high per square foot rent, units will rent at an affordable unit rent as compared to other similar unit types due to their efficient floor plans.

Further, WestPac believes that the commercial/retail component of Building 1 will add value to the entire development, not only through increased cash flow, but by providing an additional amenity to the apartment residents. The retail tenant mix will include a variety of businesses, ranging from a Subway sandwich shop to an Anytime Fitness.

The Location

Chandler Lofts is located in the Chandler Crossings area of East Lansing, Michigan, approximately 2 miles north of Michigan State University campus. Chandler Crossings is well known among Michigan State University students as an off-campus student housing destination with a high concentration of student renters and premier amenities. The site is located on the Capital Area Transit Authority (CATA) bus line, with scheduled bus service every seven minutes that delivers passengers into the heart of campus in seven minutes time.

The Project site is located within three miles of the US-127 interchange, which allows for convenient access to every major employment center, entertainment destination, or cultural attraction in the Greater Lansing Area.

About Sponsor Experience

CrowdStreet takes considerable measures to vet and understand sponsors to ensure that investors are treated with the highest degree of professionalism. To help CrowdStreet investor members more easily understand a sponsor's level of qualifications, we assign one of three designations to each sponsor. In order of experience and level of in-house capabilities, they are:

  • Emerging

    Emerging Sponsors are defined as experienced commercial real estate principals who have invested through their current entity for fewer than 5 years and, therefore, must meet additional criteria found in CrowdStreet's standard Posting Policy .

  • Seasoned
